IT Band (Iliotibial Band)

Iliotibial band (ITB) is a tendon running from hip to knee, susceptible to irritation.

ITBS occurs when the ITB becomes irritated or swollen from rubbing against hip or knee bones.

ITBS can affect one or both legs, termed bilateral ITBS.

Athletes like basketball players, cyclists, runners, and soccer players are prone to ITBS.

Risk factors include bowed legs, leg length discrepancy, knee arthritis, and certain movement patterns.

Exercises:

  • Iliotibial Band Stretch (Standing): Cross one leg in front of the other, bend down, and touch toes. Move hands across the floor toward the front leg for added stretch. Hold for 15-30 seconds, repeat 3 times, then switch legs and repeat.
  • Iliotibial Band Stretch (Side-leaning): Stand sideways near a wall, place one hand on it for support. Cross the farthest leg over the other, keeping the foot closest to the wall flat. Lean hips into the wall, hold for 15 seconds, repeat 3 times, then switch legs.
  • Standing Calf Stretch: Face a wall, place hands at eye level. Keep one leg back with heel on the floor, other leg forward. Lean into the wall, feeling the stretch in the calf. Hold for 15-30 seconds, repeat 3 times, then switch legs and repeat.
  • Hamstring Stretch on Wall: Lie on back near doorway, legs extended. Raise one leg, rest it against the wall next to the door frame. Feel the stretch in the back of the thigh, hold for 15-30 seconds. Repeat 3 times, then switch legs and repeat.
  • Quadriceps Stretch: Stand arm's length from the wall, injured leg farthest. Brace against the wall, grasp the ankle of the injured leg, pull the heel toward the buttocks. Keep knees together, hold for 15-30 seconds.
  • Wall Squat with a Ball: Stand against the wall with feet 2 feet away, shoulder-width apart. Place the ball behind the back, squat down to a 45-degree angle. Hold for 10 seconds, repeat 10 times, build up to 3 sets of 10.
  • Side-Lying Leg Lift: Lie on the uninjured side, tighten the front thigh muscles of the top leg. Lift the leg 8-10 inches away from the other leg, keep straight, lower slowly. Do 3 sets of 10 repetitions.
