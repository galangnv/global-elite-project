Hamstrings

A tear or strain in the group of muscles and tendons in the back of the thigh.

Common among athletes and can be debilitating.

Connects largest thigh muscle to bone, allowing leg flexion and extension.

Engaged in activities like running, jumping, and stretching.

Overstretching, fatigue, direct impact, inadequate warm-up, or previous injury.

Symptoms include sudden pain, tenderness, swelling, bruising, and muscle stiffness.

Exercises:

  • Hamstring Set (Heel Dig): Sit with affected leg bent, opposite leg straight and supported. Tighten hamstring by pressing heel into the floor. Hold for 6 seconds, rest for 10 seconds, repeat 8 to 12 times.
  • Hamstring Curl: Lie on stomach with knees straight, pillow under stomach. Lift affected leg by bending knee, bringing foot towards buttock. Move leg up and down, repeat 8 to 12 times. Add resistance with exercise band when comfortable.
  • Hip Extension: Stand facing a wall with hands on it. Keeping affected leg straight, kick it straight back behind you. Lower leg to the starting position, repeat 8 to 12 times. Add resistance with an exercise band when comfortable.
  • Hamstring Wall Stretch: Lie on back in a doorway with the good leg through the door. Slide affected leg up the wall to straighten the knee, feel a gentle stretch. Hold for 1 to 6 minutes, repeat 2 to 4 times. Alternatively, use a towel under the affected foot, straighten the knee, and pull back on the towel.
  • Calf Stretch: Stand facing a wall with hands on it. Place the affected leg a step behind the other leg. Keep the back leg straight, bend the front knee to feel the stretch in the calf. Hold for 15 to 30 seconds, repeat 2 to 4 times. Repeat with the back knee bent.
  • Single-Leg Balance: Stand on a flat surface with arms out to sides. Lift the good leg off the floor, bending at the knee. Balance on the affected leg for up to 30 seconds, rest for 10 seconds. Repeat 6 to 8 times. Progress to balancing with eyes closed, then on a pillow or foam.
