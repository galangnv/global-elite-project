Shoulders

Discomfort around the shoulder joint due to injury or damage.

Can manifest as weakness, aching, stiffness, soreness, or popping.

Severe pain may include sudden or sharp sensations, limited arm movement, swelling, bruising, or sleep disturbances.

Complex joint comprised of the upper arm bone (humerus), shoulder blade (scapula), and collarbone (clavicle).

Contains acromioclavicular and glenohumeral joints.

Components include acromion, bursa, coracoid, labrum, and rotator cuff.

Exercises:

  • Pendulum
    Muscles Worked: Deltoids, supraspinatus, infraspinatus, subscapularis
    Repetitions: 2 sets of 10
    Days Per Week: 5 to 6
    Directions: Lean forward with one hand on a surface for support, letting the other arm hang. Swing the arm gently forward and back, then side-to-side, and in circular motions. Repeat with the other arm, avoiding rounding the back or locking knees.
  • Crossover Arm Stretch
    Muscles Worked: Posterior deltoid
    Repetitions: 4 each side
    Days Per Week: 5 to 6
    Directions: Pull one arm across the chest, holding at the upper arm, and stretch for 30 seconds. Relax for 30 seconds and repeat with the other arm, avoiding pressure on the elbow.
  • Passive Internal Rotation
    Muscles Worked: Supraspinatus
    Repetitions: 4 each side
    Days Per Week: 5 to 6
    Directions: Hold a stick behind your back with one hand and grasp the other end lightly. Pull horizontally to stretch the shoulder, holding for 30 seconds. Repeat on the other side, avoiding leaning or twisting.
  • Passive External Rotation
    Muscles Worked: Infraspinatus, teres minor
    Repetitions: 4 each side
    Days Per Week: 5 to 6
    Directions: Grasp a stick with one hand and cup the other end with the other hand. Push horizontally to stretch the shoulder, holding for 30 seconds. Repeat on the other side, keeping hips forward and avoiding twisting.
  • Sleeper Stretch
    Muscles Worked: Infraspinatus, teres minor
    Repetitions: 4 reps, 3x a day
    Days Per Week: Daily
    Directions: Lie on the affected side with the arm bent, using the other arm to push it down gently. Hold for 30 seconds, then relax for 30 seconds, avoiding bending the wrist.
  • Standing Row
    Muscles Worked: Middle and lower trapezius
    Repetitions: 3 sets of 8
    Days Per Week: 3
    Directions: Use an elastic band attached to a stable object. Hold the band with elbow bent and pull it straight back, squeezing shoulder blades. Repeat, focusing on the back of the shoulder and upper back.
