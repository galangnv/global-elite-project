Neck Muscles (platysma, sternocleidomastoid)

Described as persistent ache, stabbing, burning, or shooting pain.

Symptoms may include headache, stiffness, inability to move or turn the neck, and numbness or tingling in shoulders or arms.

Exercises:

  • Neck rotation:
    Sit in a firm chair or stand up straight. Turn your head to the right while keeping your chin level, hold for 15 to 30 seconds. Turn your head to the left, hold for 15 to 30 seconds. Repeat 2 to 4 times to each side.
  • Neck stretches:
    Look straight ahead and tip your right ear to your right shoulder without letting your left shoulder rise, hold for 15 to 30 seconds. Tilt your head to the left without letting your right shoulder rise, hold for 15 to 30 seconds. Repeat 2 to 4 times to each side.
  • Forward neck flexion:
    Sit in a firm chair or stand up straight. Bend your head forward, hold for 15 to 30 seconds. Repeat 2 to 4 times.
  • Lateral (side) bend strengthening:
    Place your first two fingers on your right temple and gently bend your head to the side, hold for about 6 seconds. Repeat 8 to 12 times. Switch hands and repeat on the left side.
  • Forward bend strengthening:
    Place your first two fingers on your forehead and gently bend your head forward, hold for about 6 seconds. Repeat 8 to 12 times.
  • Neutral position strengthening:
    Place your fingertips on the back of your head at the top of your neck and gently bend your head backward, hold for about 6 seconds. Repeat 8 to 12 times.
  • Chin tuck:
    Lie on the floor with a rolled-up towel under your neck. Slowly bring your chin toward your chest, hold for a count of 6, then relax for up to 10 seconds. Repeat 8 to 12 times.
