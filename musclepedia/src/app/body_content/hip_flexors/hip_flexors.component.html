Hip Flexors

A hip flexor strain involves an injury to one of the hip flexor muscles, located where the thigh meets the hip.

They are a common type of muscle strain, especially among athletes.

Strains are categorized into three grades to indicate severity, and recovery typically involves rest and home treatments.

Symptoms include pain, tightness, difficulty walking, weakness, bruising, swelling, and muscle spasms.

Exercises:

  • Pelvic Tilt with Marching: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Tighten belly muscles and buttocks, press lower back to the floor. Lift and lower one leg like marching, hold each lift for 6 seconds. Repeat 8 to 12 times.
  • Scissors: Lie on back with knees bent at 90 degrees, feet off the floor. Tighten belly muscles and buttocks, press lower back to the floor. Straighten one leg about 30 cm off the floor for 6 seconds, then switch legs. Repeat 8 to 12 times.
  • Hamstring Stretch (Lying Down): Lie flat on your back with legs straight, use a towel roll under the lower back if needed. Lift affected leg straight up toward the body until feeling a stretch at the back of the thigh. Hold for at least 30 seconds, repeat 2 to 4 times.
  • Quadricep and Hip Flexor Stretch (Lying on Side): Lie on the side with the good leg flat on the floor, support head with hand. Bend top leg, reach behind to grab front of foot or ankle. Pull foot toward buttock to feel stretch in front of thigh, hold for 15 to 30 seconds. Repeat 2 to 4 times.
  • Hip Flexor Stretch (Kneeling): Kneel on the affected leg, bend the other leg in front with foot flat on the floor. Keep the back straight, push hips forward to feel the stretch in upper thigh and hip. Hold for 15 to 30 seconds, repeat 2 to 4 times.
  • Hip Flexor Stretch (Edge of Table): Lie flat on your back on a table or flat bench, knees and lower legs hanging off the edge. Grab the good leg at the knee, pull knee back toward the chest. Let affected leg hang down until feeling a stretch in upper thigh and hip. Hold for 15 to 30 seconds, repeat 2 to 4 times.
