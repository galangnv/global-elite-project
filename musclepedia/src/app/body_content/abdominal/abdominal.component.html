Abdominal Muscles

An abdominal muscle strain, also known as a pulled stomach muscle, occurs when muscles in the stomach are torn or overly stretched.

Abdominal muscles support the trunk, hold organs in place, and aid movement.

Groups of abdominal muscles include oblique muscles, rectus abdominis muscles, and transversus abdominis muscles.

Abdominal muscle strains can affect any abdominal muscle, including the left side, right side, lower abdomen, or upper abdomen.

Exercises:

  • Tummy Tuck:
    Lie on your back with knees bent and fingers just inside your hip bones. Inhale deeply, then exhale while pulling your belly button toward your spine, engaging lower belly muscles. Hold for 6 seconds, then relax for up to 10 seconds. Repeat 8 to 12 times, gradually increasing hold duration and frequency.
  • Pelvic Tilt:
    Lie on your back with knees bent, brace your stomach, and press lower back into the floor. Hold for 6 seconds while breathing smoothly, then relax. Repeat 8 to 12 times to rock hips and pelvis back and forth.
  • Curl-Up:
    Lie with knees bent, arms at sides, and feet flat on the floor. Lift head and shoulders 8 to 10 centimeters, raising arms to thigh level simultaneously. Hold for 6 seconds, then relax and return to starting position. Repeat 8 to 12 times.
  • Diagonal Curl-Up:
    Lie down with knees bent and arms at sides, feet flat on the floor. Lift head and shoulders while reaching to one side with both arms. Hold for 6 seconds, then relax and return to starting position.
